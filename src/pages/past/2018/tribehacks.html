About US

tribeHacks is the College of William and Mary's annual hackathon. It's a chance for students to come together for three days to learn some new stuff, build cool technologies, and win prizes.



Who's Invited?

tribeHacks is open to all students who are interested in technology and spending the weekend building hardware or software. All experience levels and all areas of interest are welcome.



Venue

tribeHacks IV will be held in Earl Gregg Swem Library. The main library on campus, Swem is home to 1,926,900 books, over 1,000,000 visitors per year, and one of the most beloved coffee shops on campus. Swem is a great place for collaboration, and we can't wait to fill it up with hackers. Look forward to lots of open tables and comfy couches! Check out the Map



Hardware

Available hardware includes: Amazon Alexa Devices, Oculus Rifts, Arduinos and Raspberry Pis, and Drones, and more!

Experience?
TribeHacks is open to hackers of all experience levels. If you're new, we'll have workshops and some challenges to get you started.
Prizes?
We're offering over $2000 in prizes this year. For more details, including a breakdown for each prize, check out our Devpost Details coming soon.
Age?
Any current college student, or anyone who has graduated in the last 12 months is eligible to attend TribeHacks.
Teams?
Don't worry if you don't have a team yet. Meeting new people is half the fun - we'll help you find someone to work with! Teams can have a maximum of 4 people.
Preparations?
Come with ideas, but no code can be written prior to the event.
Rules?
We want this to be a safe and fun event for everyone. All participants are expected to agree to the MLH Code of Conduct.
